II. 3333/444/THE SIGNS

The Sun's annual journey through the zodiac structures the way humans see the seasons. Signs act as time markers. For example, the Sun's entrance into the sign of Aries aligns with the Spring Equinox in the Northern Hemisphere (NH) and Autumn Equinox in the Southern Hemisphere (SH) and vice versa for Libra. The Summer Solstice for NH and Winter Solstice for SH aligns with the Sun's entrance into Cancer and vice versa for Capricorn.

These four signs (Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn) mark the beginning of a new season. They are called cardinal signs, signifying initation. The signs that mark the middle of a season are called fixed signs, signifying stabilization. The signs that end a season are called mutable signs, signifying transition.

Cardinal, fixed, and mutable are all descriptors refering to the modality of the signs or their quadruplicity, a way of dividing the signs into three groups of four. Modality articulates the changing seasons in reference to the position of the Sun as it moves through the zodiac: the Sun is furthest North in Cancer, furthest South in Capricorn, furthest East in Aries, and furthest west in Libra.

Elements are another way of grouping the signs. There are four elements:

There are 3 signs in every element. 3 fire signs, 3 water signs, 3 air signs, and 3 earth signs:

  • Fire
    • Aries
    • Leo
    • Sagittarius
  • Earth
    • Capricorn
    • Taurus
    • Virgo
  • Air
    • Libra
    • Aquarius
    • Gemini
  • Water
    • Cancer
    • Scorpio
    • Pisces

Note the signs sharing an element do not ever share modality. Each element has every modality represented. We can think of the elements as 4 sets of 3 modalities known as triplicity.

Cardinal Fixed Mutable
Aries Leo Sagittarius
Capricorn Taurus Virgo
Libra Aquarius Gemini
Cancer Scorpio Pisces

Triplicity helps us differentiate between signs sharing an element. For example, we can liken Aries (Cardinal Fire) to the ignition of fire, Leo (Fixed Fire) to steady sources of heat, and Sagittarius (Mutable Fire) to the portability of fire.
